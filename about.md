I am a Senior Software Engineer at <a target="_blank"
href="https://www.trailofbits.com">Trail of Bits</a>, where I worked on
the DARPA's <a target="_blank"
href="https://aicyberchallenge.com">AI Cyber Challenge (AIxCC)</a>, helping
design and build <a target="_blank" href="https://github.com/trailofbits/buttercup">Buttercup</a>,
our autonomous Cyber Reasoning System that placed 2nd in the finals ($3M award).

I'm passionate about applying research, automation, and AI to advance software
security. My background includes the
<a target="_blank"
href="https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/cyber-grand-challenge">DARPA Cyber Grand
Challenge</a> (UC Berkeley, Prof. Dawn Song) and years contributing to
open-source security tools like
<a target="_blank" href="https://rada.re">radare2</a>,
<a target="_blank" href="https://rizin.re">rizin</a>, and
<a target="_blank" href="https://www.sigstore.dev">sigstore</a> — I'm a core
contributor to Rizin.

I got my M.Sc degree in Computer Science in February 2017, at Università degli
Studi di Milano, Italy, with a final grade of 110/110 cum laude. My thesis was
about *Blazar*, a system to automatically disassemble, analyze and instrument
CGC binaries by using the LLVM Intermediate Representation.

I got my B.Sc degree in Computer Science in October 2013, at Università degli
Studi di Milano, Italy, with a final grade of 110/110 cum Laude. My B.Sc thesis
is titled "Studio e implementazione di un integrity-checker hardware-assisted"
and it can be found [here]({{ site.url }}/static/files/bsc_thesis.pdf).
